Typographical elements

Typographical elements used through out the website Mainly consists of the use of sizings and positioning of headers and text.
To draw a users attention to specific areas of the page, allowed it to become more clear where key parts of the page are.
Spacing was used in large portions of text to allow the user to have a visual rest when reading larger portions of text and were not centered
to make sure the users attention was not entirely focus on a large chunk of text but rather the area of the page they would have come for, i.e: the encoders.
The use of italics, bold and underlining was rarely or never used, and was only used in one or two places.

Presentational elements

Presentational elements used through out the website, mainly focused on similarly mentioned methods as above, focusing on white spacing to aids users viewing of the page through the use of the 'br' tag.
The 'hr' tag was used to show a clear division between ciphers.

Visual content was added to help make the site look less plain and boring, with a header being consistent in every page and the ciphers page having visual aids focused around a specific cipher. A navigation bar was placed at the top of every page, below the header, so that it was easy to use and find, along with adding visual appeal.
